Coupon site

Redesign.

Brief

SimplyBestCoupons is a website where users can find good deals and receive cashback on their purchases from a large number of stores and brands.

The owner of the website wanted a responsive layout based on Bootstrap that would bring his website up to date with his competitors.

My role

I worked alone on this project.

My responsibility was to identify the issues with the website, provide solutions or suggestions for improvement, and create deliverables that were handed off to the website owner.

Like most people, I like to search for deals and coupons. With cashback shopping as an added bonus, every price sensible shopper would sign up immediatelly, right?

Well, not quite. After very brief interaction with the site, my initial reaction was to go somewhere else. Interestingly enough, the reason weren't the obvious design related issues.

No, the main reason for the "chop", despite the thousands of deals and cashback stores, was that I lost interest after not being able to understand navigation, or find interesting deals while looking at the homepage.

Jakob Nielsen's article, "How Long Do Users Stay on Web Pages?", elaborates how visitors tend to leave if the value proposition is not clearly communicated within the first few to ten seconds.

Old design - Source: Web Archive 2019.
The Problem

How can we fix usability of the website?

Competitive research

To gain a better understanding of UX/UI and user journey, I opened an account on several major competitor sites such as RetailMeNot, Rakuten, Groupon, and Coupons.com.

Key learning points

  • Competitors had clear value proposition (clean page layout, representation of coupons).

  • Navigating through competitors websites was easier.

  • Competitors had a good use of colors and fonts to create emotional bond with the visitor.

  • Competitors websites were responsive and mobile friendly.

Using Bootstrap framework, getting a responsive layout was relatively easy. I proceeded with:

  • Reworking the main navigation menu, instead of using tabs, changed to links with dropdowns.

  • Cleaning up the layout so it's easy to understand at a glance.

  • Adding hero areas to promote marketing offers.

  • Using color theory to create positive emotional response from the visitors, highlighting important areas like featured coupons and stores, using soft neutral colors for background to make content more prominent.

  • Reworking the copy of 3 important steps visitor needs to understand how the service works.

New design

User's account pages

When the user logs in, there were even more issues with how the data was presented. I proceeded with:

  • Adding the notification feature with a dropdown.

  • Removing the sidebar navigation, and moving the links into Profile block in top right corner pf the header.

  • Creating a cleaner, glanceable interface for cashback summary and other pages.

  • Designing signup / login / register / account verification journey.

Branding

As stated in Kelley Gordon's, "Using Color to Enhance Your Design", article:

The choice of a color palette and its application to a design are not mere happy accidents. It takes iteration and careful application to really benefit and take full advantage of color in a UI. Appropriate color use can enhance brand perception, draw attention and encourage interactions, impact users emotions, and increase usability.

Color variations

Proposed color scheme wasn't to site owner's personal liking. He requested several options to choose from. I decided to conduct a user interview.

Testing

The target group consisted of 11 people, a mix of men and women, from 26 to 50 years of age. The interview consisted of a single question:

"Which color variation of the homepage do you prefer?"

Majority of interviewed audience (6) preferred gradient version, purple was a popular choice for 3 people, whilst blue and dark gray seemed to be a niche choice.

54.54% preferred gradient variation
27.27% preferred purple variation
9.09% preferred blue variation
9.09% preferred dark gray variation

With data confirming my hypothesis, I was able to convince the site owner to keep the gradient variation.

Variations used in testing

Conclusion

Although the client couldn't provide before / after data, he mentioned several people reaching out, commenting how they liked the new design.

By improving the usability and applying design principles, number of happy customers increased, resulting in more traffic, and more revenue for the business.

Key learning points

  • A lot of people get to coupon deals through email campaigns on their phones, so it's important to provide good mobile experience.

  • Clear communication of the value proposition helps with keeping users interested in the website or service.

  • Effective navigation reduces cognitive load and helps users to complete their tasks quicker.

  • Users emotionally respond to colors and font choices. Following good practices based on color theory and specific markets can be crucial for overall experience.

  • Distinctive logo promotes brand awareness.
