Coupon site

Redesign.

Brief

SimplyBestCoupons is a website where users can find good deals and receive cashback on their purchases from a large number of stores and brands.

The owner of the website wanted a responsive layout based on Bootstrap that would bring his website up to date with his competitors.

My role

I worked alone on this project.

My responsibility was to identify the issues with the website, provide solutions or suggestions for improvement, and create deliverables that were handed off to the website owner.

Like most people, I like to search for deals and coupons. With cashback shopping as an added bonus, every price sensible shopper would sign up immediatelly, right?

Well, not quite. After very brief interaction with the site, my initial reaction was to go somewhere else. The reason wasn't because the site wasn't responsive, or that the color scheme was weird, nor that the logo and branding were bland, nor that the flow of text, font weights and spacing lacked order and consistency.

No, the main reason for the "chop", despite the thousands of deals and cashback stores, was that I couldn't see benefits on the homepage.

Jakob Nielsen's article, "How Long Do Users Stay on Web Pages?", elaborates how visitors tend to leave if the value proposition is not clearly communicated within the first few to ten seconds.

Old Design - Source: Web Archive 2019.

Competitive research

To gain a better understanding of UX/UI and user journey, I opened an account on several major competitor sites such as RetailMeNot, Rakuten, Groupon, and Coupons.com.

Key learning points

Compared to the competitors:

  • Provides the opportunity to test the interaction of different elements, and fix the issues on the spot.

  • Enables rapid delivery of different layouts, in a form of interactive pages, for user testing.

  • Design system can be re-used in other projects with minimal changes.

The Problem

How can we make this product more memorable?

There is no clear diferentiation between the content. I'm finding the uniformness & color scheme of the website to be frustrating.

Branding is not memorable, and hardly distinctive from the rest of the website. Why would people use this website, if they can't remember it?

New Design

The Solution

  • Making it easier for the users to navigate through the site helps them to do their tasks quicker.

  • Distinctive logo promotes brand awareness.

  • A lot of people get to coupon deals through email campaigns on their phones, so it's important to provide good mobile experience.

  • Having a color scheme that makes the user feel comfortable, helps with the overall experience, and could make tasks feel less frustrating.

Variations

Site owner wasn't sure about the proposed color scheme, so he asked for several other examples.

Testing

The target group consisted of 11 people, a mix of men and women, from 26 to 50 years of age. The interview consisted of a single question:

"Which color variation do you prefer?"

Majority of interviewed audience (6) preferred gradient version, purple was a popular choice for 3 people, whilst blue and dark gray seemed to be a niche choice.

54.54% preferred gradient variation
27.27% preferred purple variation
9.09% preferred blue variation
9.09% preferred dark gray variation
Variations used in testing

Conclusion

  • A lot of people get to coupon deals through email campaigns on their phones, so it's important to provide good mobile experience.

  • Clear communication of the value proposition helps with keeping users' interest in the website or service.

  • Effective navigation reduces cognitive load and helps users to complete their tasks quicker.

  • Selecting a good color scheme emotionally connects with the users' overall experience.

  • Distinctive logo promotes brand awareness.

Although the website owner couldn't provide before / after data, he mentioned several people reaching out, commenting how they liked the new design.

This project was a success because by improving the usability and using design principles, number of delighted users increased, resulting in more traffic, and more revenue for the business.
